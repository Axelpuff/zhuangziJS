Yang Zhu is a figure who survives only in the caricatures of other writers, and he may potentially be mythological. These portrayals suggest that Yang Zhu supported self-satisfaction, on the basis that following one's nature (*xing*) aligns with Heaven (*tian*). The emergence of Yang Zhu marked a turning point in Chinese classical philosophy: human nature, which Confucius had refused to speak of, now took center stage.
